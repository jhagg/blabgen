PRIVACY POLICY – VISITORS

 

WHAT IS PERSONAL DATA?

Personal data is any information that can be used, alone or together with other data, to uniquely identify any living human being, such as your name, or any email addresses or phone numbers related to you personally.

 

WHAT PERSONAL DATA DO WE COLLECT AND USE, FOR WHAT PURPOSES AND ON WHAT LEGAL GROUNDS?

If you visit Axis’ premises.

Contact details. Typically your name, email address(es) and phone number(s).

Employment details. Typically the name and contact details of your employer and your job title.

Other data that you intentionally share. Information about you that you voluntarily submit to us in for example free text fields, web forms and other contexts.

Video, photos and audio material. Video, photos and audio recorded at Axis’ premises by network video camera and software products under development.

 

As applicable, we use this personal data for the following purposes:

Legal grounds for our collection and use of your personal data:

To keep a record of who is entering and leaving the premises.

This use, including the collection of the personal data needed to do it, is necessary for the purpose of our legitimate interest to maintain a visitors record and control who is currently visiting the premises.

To do other things that you have explicitly asked us to do, for example if you chose to opt in to one or more specific activities that we offer.

If you have opted in to a specific activity that we offer, this use, including the collection of the personal data needed to do it, is necessary for the performance of the contract we have with you – that is, our obligation to provide to you or enable you to take part in the offered activity.

Video, photos and (if specifically stated) audio material is processed in order to test the functionality and features of Axis’ network video camera and software products.

The processing is necessary for the purpose of Axis’ legitimate interest to test the functionality and features of its products, as part of Axis’ research and development work.

If Axis has obtained your consent prior to the recording, the legal ground is that consent.

Video, photos and (if specifically stated) audio material is processed to demonstrate Axis’ products and technology and to market Axis as a company and its business.

The processing is necessary for the purpose of Axis’ legitimate interest to demonstrate Axis’ products and technology and to market Axis as a company and its business.

If you have entered into a modelling agreement with Axis, the legal basis is that the processing is necessary to fulfil the contract with you.

Video material is also processed to maintain security at the Axis premises.

The processing is necessary for the purpose of Axis’ legitimate interest in maintaining security at the Axis premises.

 

Axis’ core business is the development of network video solutions for professional IP video surveillance. As part of the development process, it is of great importance to test the functionality and features of some of our network video camera and software products. We do so by installing products under development at various places at our premises. Axis has concluded that such testing activities are necessary for the success of Axis’ business, and that it brings great value to our research and development work. This means that when visiting Axis, and by moving around at our premises, you may take part in the development process of Axis’ products. Whenever Axis is conducting video recordings in such areas, ongoing recordings will be indicated by signs, directing you to this privacy notice.

If not specifically indicated otherwise, Axis will only record video and photo material. Audio recordings will be subject to additional information signs and will only be conducted in closed off spaces dedicated for that purpose. You will not be subject to audio recordings unless you choose to enter such clearly marked recording areas.

The material recorded for R&D purposes will never be processed in order to analyze the behaviour or track the pattern of movement of a certain individual (except strictly for R&D purposes) but is limited to the testing and evaluation of features and functionality of the Axis products, such as resolution, audio and video compression, operating conditions and audio streaming.

When possible, we will obtain the consent for processing for R&D purposes from all concerned individuals (instead of relying on legitimate interest). In such cases, the legal basis for such processing is the consent of the individual.

In some cases, we may use recordings for marketing purposes. In case we will use these recordings for commercials or printed marketing material, we will obtain the prior approval from you to use such material or enter into a model agreement. The legal basis for our processing will be our legitimate interest to demonstrate Axis’ products and technology and to market Axis as a company and its business. If you have entered into a modelling agreement with Axis the legal basis is that the processing is necessary to fulfil the contract.

 

WITH WHOM DO WE SHARE YOUR PERSONAL DATA?

Axis do not sell your personal data. However, Axis operates globally and we may share your personal data with our group of companies and business partners – but only for the purposes described in this privacy notice.

Personal data encompassed by the GDPR may be transferred to organizations in countries outside the European Union and the European Economic Area. We will not transfer any of your personal data to organizations in countries outside of the European Union and the European Economic Area (third country) without ensuring appropriate safeguards for the protection of your personal data, for example the existence of a so called ‘adequacy decision’ by the Commission or the inclusion of the so-called ‘standard contractual clauses’ (as approved by the European Commission from time to time) in a contract between us and the third country recipient company of your personal data.

Video, photos and audio recordings collected for R&D purposes will be used by Axis internally and will not be distributed to any third party. Only those employees and consultants of Axis who are directly involved in the development of Axis products, and with a need to process the recorded material in order to fulfil their work tasks, will have access to the recorded material.

Video recordings collected for security purposes will be used by Axis internally and will not be distributed to any third party, unless Axis need to provide information at the request of authorities for further investigation.

Video, photos and audio recordings collected for marketing purposes may be distributed to the public for the purposes of demonstrating our products and technology and to market our company and business.

 

HOW LONG DO WE KEEP YOUR PERSONAL DATA?

General.

We will not use your personal data for any longer than needed in order to fulfil the purposes described in this privacy notice. After that, we will delete or permanently de-identify your personal data unless required or entitled by law to retain it, or parts of it.

Personal data in video- and audio recordings.

Axis will only store the video- and audio material recorded for security purposes for 3 days before being deleted, unless there is an incident captured which need further investigation.

Axis will only store video, photos and audio recorded for R&D or marketing purposes, which is recorded at Axis’ premises and which has not been anonymized, for 6 months after it has been recorded. After that, it will be deleted from Axis’ servers, unless needed for further R&D or marketing purposes.

Axis will only store the video, photos and audio material recorded outside Axis’ premises and which has not been anonymized for 2 months after it has been recorded. After that, it will be deleted from Axis’ servers, unless needed for further R&D purposes or marketing purposes (as applicable).

WHAT ARE YOUR RIGHTS IN RELATION TO YOUR PERSONAL DATA HELD BY US?

If we process your personal data you have several rights as set out below. Axis value your privacy and recognize the importance of protecting your personal data and will never discriminate you for exercising any of these rights.

You can always verify and make corrections and additions to your personal data that are available when signed in to your Axis online account (go to your profile). You have a right to get information on what personal data we have about you, access this data, request that we erase certain parts or all of your personal data, request that we restrict the use of or stop using your personal data, and have your personal data resubmitted to you in a structured and accessible format.

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, go to www.axis.com/privacy and submit your request (also available when you are signed in to your Axis online account). If you have any questions regarding your personal data that you believe were not answered in this privacy notice, you may contact our data protection office at dataprotection@axis.com.

Please note that if you ask us to erase or restrict the use of any of your personal data, we may not be able to continue managing your Axis online account and/or providing services to you.

Please also note that if you want us to erase or restrict the use of personal data relating to video- and audio recordings, you should specify the time and place for such recording to make it possible for us to amend or remove sequences involving such personal data.

If you have given your written consent to the processing, you have the right to withdraw the consent provided by you at any time by contacting us. If your consent is withdrawn, we will no longer process your personal data for the purpose that you had given your consent to.

Should you be dissatisfied with our collection and use of your personal data, please let us know and we will do our best to address your complaints. Your privacy is very important to us, and we always strive to protect and secure your personal data in the best possible way. Should you feel that we have failed in this ambition, you are entitled to lodge a complaint with the Swedish Data Protection Authority (Sw., Datainspektionen) or other relevant supervisory authority, or such other supervisory authority as may be determined in the future.
